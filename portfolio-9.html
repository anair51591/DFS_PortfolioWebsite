Radha and Madhava

(1890)


Radha and Madhava is a scene that portrays the love between Lord Krishna, also fondly called Madhava, and Radha. Radha is seen as equal to Krishna, the other half of his soul. Additionally, Varma conveys a deeper meaning through this painting. He portrays the feminine nature in a man, with hips that are heavily curved and the masculine traits in a woman with arms and feet open, a style, very unorthodox for its time
